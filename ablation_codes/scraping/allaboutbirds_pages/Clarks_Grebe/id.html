The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    A large waterbird with a very long, slender neck, a fine, straight bill, and a relatively small body.

    Relative Size

    Larger and more slender than a Mallard, much smaller and slighter than a Common Loon.

    crow-sized
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
      • Length: 21.6-29.5 in (55-75 cm)
      • Weight: 25.3-44.4 oz (718-1258 g)
      • Wingspan: 22.8-25.2 in (58-64 cm)
    © Scott Somershoe | Macaulay Library
  • Color Pattern

    A neat combination of blackish above and white below. The face is white up to and around the eye, where it meets the black cap. The bill is vivid orange or orange-yellow, and at close range the eye is red.

    © Nancy Christensen | Macaulay Library
  • Behavior

    Clark’s Grebes spend much of the day diving for prey or resting on the water’s surface. Courting birds pair off for ritualized displays that involve synchronized racing across the water in a nearly vertical position. These birds fly only infrequently and usually migrate at night.

    © Galatee Films | Macaulay Library
  • Habitat

    Western Grebes are rarely found away from aquatic habitats. Breeding birds use freshwater lakes; nonbreeding birds are found in both freshwater and along saltwater coasts.

    © Myles McNally | Macaulay Library

Regional Differences

There are two subspecies: mostly migratory transitionalis of the United States and Canada, which is larger, with longer wings; and clarkii, which breeds only on the Mexican Plateau.
