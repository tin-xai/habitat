Black-and-white Warbler

Adult male

Black-and-white Warblers have a striped (not solid) crown and a black (not white) cheek. They also creep up and down trees unlike Blackpoll Warblers.

© Ryan Schain | Macaulay LibraryMassachusetts, May 06, 2017

Similar Species

Cape May Warbler

Adult female/immature male

Females and immature Cape May Warblers have finer streaking on the throat and are often yellower below than Blackpoll Warblers.

© Lucas Berrigan | Macaulay LibraryNova Scotia, September 01, 2016
View Full Species Account
Similar Species

Bay-breasted Warbler

Nonbreeding female/immature male

Nonbreeding Bay-breasted Warblers have thicker wingbars with darker feathers between them than Blackpoll Warblers, and they usually show chestnut-washed flanks.

© Ian Davies | Macaulay LibraryMassachusetts, December 27, 2015
View Full Species Account
Similar Species

Blackburnian Warbler

Female/immature

Female/immature Blackburnian Warblers have a triangular ear patch and tend to have an orangish wash on the face and throat that Blackpolls lack.

© William Higgins | Macaulay LibraryVirginia, September 15, 2016
View Full Species Account
Similar Species

Pine Warbler

Adult female

Female Pine Warblers have unstreaked upperparts unlike Blackpoll Warblers and a brighter yellow throat.

© Ryan Schain | Macaulay LibraryMassachusetts, May 09, 2016

Blackpoll Warbler

  • Breeding male

    Small songbird with a short tail and a small thin bill. Breeding males have a black cap, a white cheek, and black streaking on the back and flanks. Note orangish legs.

    © Simon Boivin | Macaulay LibraryQuebec, May 29, 2017
  • Breeding female

    Breeding females look similar to males, but have a finely streaked cap. Note broken eyering, dark eyeline, and orangish legs.

    © Brian Sullivan | Macaulay LibraryAlaska, June 30, 2019
  • Breeding male

    © Eric Liner | Macaulay LibraryAlaska, June 23, 2007
    Not all videos have sound
  • Breeding female

    Some breeding females have a yellow wash on the breast and head. Note finely streaked cap, broken eyering, dark eyeline, and orangish legs.

    © Donna Pomeroy | Macaulay LibraryAlaska, June 09, 2017
  • Nonbreeding female/immature

    Nonbreeding females/immatures are washed in pale yellow with blurry streaking on the flanks. Note bold white wingbars.
