The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    Small, long-bodied bird with a small head and bill. Flies on long and relatively broad, pointed wings. Note square tail.

    Relative Size

    Larger than Ruby-throated Hummingbird, smaller than a Purple Martin.

    sparrow-sized or smaller
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
      • Length: 4.7-5.9 in (12-15 cm)
      • Weight: 0.3-0.6 oz (10-18 g)
      • Wingspan: 10.6-11.8 in (27-30 cm)
    © Kyle Lima | Macaulay Library
  • Color Pattern

    Entirely brown above with a dingy throat and chest that fades to white. Sexes similar. Juveniles look like adults, but have cinnamon wingbars.

    © Ian Hearn | Macaulay Library
  • Behavior

    Twists and turns low above water bodies and open areas, taking insects in midair. Flies with slower and more deliberate wingbeats than other swallows. Often perches on wires and exposed branches to preen.

    © Benjamin Clock | Macaulay Library
  • Habitat

    Found in open areas often near water and nests in burrows excavated by other animals as well as in crevices in gutters, boxes, drainpipes, walls, and bridges.

    © Spencer Follett | Macaulay Library
