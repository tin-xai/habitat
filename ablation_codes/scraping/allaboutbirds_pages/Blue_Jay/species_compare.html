Steller's Jay

Adult (Interior)

Steller's Jays don't overlap much with Blue Jays. Their dark crest and underparts quickly separate them from the blue crested and pale underparts of Blue Jays.

© Doug Hitchcox | Macaulay LibraryArizona, January 16, 2017

Woodhouse's Scrub-Jay

Adult (Woodhouse's)

Woodhouse's Scrub-Jays rarely overlap with Blue Jays, but they can readily be separated by the lack of a crest, white wingbars, and black necklace.

Blue Jay

  • Adult

    Blue above and white below, with a prominent crest and a bold black necklace. The wings and tail are barred with black, and it has a bold white wingbar.

    © Scott Martin | Macaulay LibraryMassachusetts, November 03, 2017
  • Adult

    © Ryan Sanderson | Macaulay LibraryIndiana, December 23, 2020
  • Adult (with Red-winged Blackbird)

    Blue Jays often come to feeders where they eat many types of seeds, especially sunflower. They hold seeds between their feet and hammer them open. Note the mostly blue tail with white tips, which can be distinctive in flight.

    © Eric Liner | Macaulay LibraryNew York, May 01, 2006
    Not all videos have sound
  • Adult

    © Daniel Jauvin | Macaulay LibraryQuebec, December 24, 2016
  • Adult

    Large white tail corners are prominent in flight, along with a black necklace and white underparts.

    © Steve Kolbe | Macaulay LibraryMichigan, May 19, 2017
  • Adult

    Jays are noisy birds; this one is visibly agitated and calling frequently along with chickadees, cardinals, woodpeckers, nuthatches, and other birds. It's likely they've discovered an owl or hawk and are mobbing it to try to make it leave.

    © Jay McGowan | Macaulay LibraryNew York, November 14, 2014
    Not all videos have sound
  • Adult

    When viewed head on the black necklace and feathering around the base of the bill are especially prominent.

    © Ryan Sanderson | Macaulay LibraryIndiana, December 08, 2019
  • Adult

    © Diane St-Jacques | Macaulay LibraryQuebec, November 12, 2019
  • Male and female building nest

    Nests are made mostly of twigs and well hidden in trees. Both sexes build the nest; the female usually spends more time arranging twigs and lining the nest with rootlets, grass, and sometimes mud.

    © Timothy Barksdale | Macaulay LibraryNew York, May 01, 2004
    Not all videos have sound
  • Adult

    Often visits feeders, sometimes in flocks.

    © Suzanne Labbé | Macaulay LibraryQuebec, January 07, 2017
  • Habitat

    © Joe Wujcik | Macaulay LibraryAlabama, December 09, 2019
  • Adult

    Note the strong, straight bill and the crest which can be raised or lowered at will. The pale-blue iridescent patches in the wing can glitter or look dull, depending on the angle of light.

    © Eric Liner | Macaulay LibraryNew York, May 01, 2006
