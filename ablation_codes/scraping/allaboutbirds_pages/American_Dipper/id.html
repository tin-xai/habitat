The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    Medium-sized, round-bodied, and chunky, with long legs, rounded head and short neck, a short, sturdy bill, and a short tail.

    Relative Size

    Larger and heavier-bodied than a Western Bluebird, smaller than an American Robin.

    robin-sized
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
    © Kim Beardmore | Macaulay Library
  • Color Pattern

    Adults are dark gray all over, slightly more brownish around the head. Legs are pink and the bill is black. Young birds have faintly barred underparts and a yellowish bill. Look for distinct white eyelids when they blink.

    © Mary Rumple | Macaulay Library
  • Behavior

    Wades and swims in turbulent rocky streams. Dives beneath the water head-first with wings slightly open to forage for insects by walking on the streambed. Frequently bobs its entire body by repeatedly crouching and standing.

    © Evan Barrientos | Macaulay Library
  • Habitat

    Fast flowing, rocky streams with gravel or rocky bottoms. Perches on boulders and woody debris.

    © Dan Vickers | Macaulay Library
