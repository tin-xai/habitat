The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    A large, lanky bird with a long tail, long, full wings, and a fairly thick, curved bill.

    Relative Size

    Larger than a Northern Mockingbird, smaller than a Common Grackle.

    between robin and crow
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
    © Jim de Waal Malefyt | Macaulay Library
  • Color Pattern

    Brown above, buffy below, especially on the belly, with a dark mask, yellow or gray eyering, and bold black-and-white pattern on the underside of the tail. The bill is mostly black, with the lower (basal) portion yellow.

    © ryan doherty | Macaulay Library
  • Behavior

    Perches in tree canopies and watches carefully for insects, lizards, and other prey, then hops or flies quickly at prey once spotted. “Tenderizes” larger prey by beating it against a branch. Often sits motionless for long periods.

    © Gregory Griffith | Macaulay Library
  • Habitat

    Red mangrove and black mangrove stands in Florida, as well as in hardwood hammocks and beach scrub, usually near mangroves. In Caribbean and Latin America, look for them in scrubby woodlands, cultivated areas, rainforest, and cloud forest.

    © Maynor Ovando | Macaulay Library
