The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    Brown Thrashers are fairly large, slender songbirds with long proportionsâ€”the legs are long and sturdy, and the bill is long and slightly downcurved. The tail is long, too, and often cocked upward in the manner of wrens.

    Relative Size

    Slightly smaller than a Blue Jay; slightly larger than a Northern Mockingbird.

    robin-sized
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
      • Length: 9.1-11.8 in (23-30 cm)
      • Weight: 2.1-3.1 oz (61-89 g)
      • Wingspan: 11.4-12.6 in (29-32 cm)
    Â© Nathan Dubrow | Macaulay Library
  • Color Pattern

    Brown Thrashers are foxy brown birds with heavy, dark streaking on their whitish underparts. The face is gray-brown and the wings show two black-and-white wingbars. They have bright-yellow eyes.

    Â© Simon Best | Macaulay Library
  • Behavior

    Brown Thrashers skulk in shrubby tangles or forage on the ground below dense cover. Theyâ€™re most obvious when they sing their loud songs from shrubs and treetops. The song is a complex string of many musical phrases (many copied from other birdsâ€™ songs, with each phrase typically sung twice before moving on). They also make a distinctive, harsh tsuck note.

    Â© Benjamin Clock | Macaulay Library
  • Habitat

    Scrubby fields, dense regenerating woods, and forest edges are the primary habitats of Brown Thrashers. They rarely venture far from thick undergrowth into which they can easily retreat.

    Â© Christopher McPherson | Macaulay Library

Regional Differences

Brown Thrashers from the western Great Plains are slightly larger and paler than those breeding farther east.
