Least Grebe

Breeding adult

Least Grebes are smaller with thinner bills than Pied-billed Grebes, which have a thick and stubby bill. Least Grebes have yellow eyes while Pied-billed Grebes have a dark eye.

© Aurelio Molina Hernández | Macaulay LibraryVeracruz, July 15, 2017

Similar Species

Horned Grebe

Nonbreeding

Horned Grebes have a thinner bill than Pied-billed Grebes. Nonbreeding Horned Grebes have white on the chest and face whereas Pied-billed Grebes are brownish overall without white patches.

© Tim Kleman | Macaulay LibraryOhio, March 18, 2017
View Full Species Account
Similar Species

Eared Grebe

Nonbreeding

Nonbreeding Eared Grebes have a white patch on the back of the head and a whitish neck whereas Pied-billed Grebes are brownish overall and lack white patches. Note bill size differences; thin on Eared and stubby on Pied-billed.

© Charmaine Anderson | Macaulay LibraryOntario, January 16, 2017

Female/nonbreeding male

    © Brad Imhoff | Macaulay LibraryOhio, March 26, 2020
