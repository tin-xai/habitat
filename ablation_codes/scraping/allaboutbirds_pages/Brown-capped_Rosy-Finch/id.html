The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    A small, compact songbird with a conical bill, relatively short tail, and rather long wings.

    Relative Size

    Larger than an American Goldfinch, smaller than a Mountain Bluebird.

    sparrow-sized or smaller
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
      • Length: 5.5-6.3 in (14-16 cm)
      • Weight: 0.8-1.2 oz (23-33 g)
      • Wingspan: 13.0 in (33 cm)
    © Joan Baker | Macaulay Library
  • Color Pattern

    Adult male in breeding plumage is mostly rich brown above, deep rosy below, with rosy edges to wings and rump, charcoal gray cap and tail, and dark bill and legs. Adult female similarly patterned but much less richly colored. Bill is black in breeding season and yellow in winter. Juvenile brownish overall with strikingly patterned wings that may show a hint of rosy edges.

    © Jacob Drucker | Macaulay Library
  • Behavior

    In summer, forages near the melting edges of snowfields. Walks or hops slowly to pick seeds, insects, or spiders off the ground (including snowfields, glaciers, frozen lakes), and sometimes pursues insects in flight. In winter, large flocks often move into inhabited areas, seeking food around buildings and roadways.

    © Timothy Barksdale | Macaulay Library
  • Habitat

    Nests in cliffs and other rocky environments in alpine meadows of the Rocky Mountains. Winters in the same environments but often descends to lower elevations, including forested habitats, during bad weather.

    © Jeremiah Trimble | Macaulay Library
