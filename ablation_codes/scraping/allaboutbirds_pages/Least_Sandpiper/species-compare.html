Semipalmated Sandpiper

Nonbreeding adult

Semipalmated Sandpipers are slightly larger with stouter bills than Least Sandpipers and black (not yellow) legs. They tend to have grayer backs than Least Sandpipers, which are often browner.

© Jonathan Eckerson | Macaulay LibraryMassachusetts, July 16, 2017

Western Sandpiper

Nonbreeding adult

Western Sandpipers are slightly larger with stouter bills and black legs instead of the Least Sandpiper’s yellowish legs. Nonbreeding Western Sandpipers tend to have a whiter breast than the brownish breast on Least Sandpipers.

© Patrick Maurice | Macaulay LibraryFlorida, November 06, 2016

Breeding

Pectoral Sandpipers have yellow legs like Least Sandpipers, but they are much larger with a dark shield of streaking on the chest.

Least Sandpiper

  • Breeding adult

    Small sandpiper with yellow legs and a thin slightly curved bill. Breeding birds have rusty speckling on the back.

    © Simon Boivin | Macaulay LibraryQuebec, May 21, 2020
  • Nonbreeding adult

    Small sandpiper with yellow legs. Nonbreeding birds are brownish with a smudgy brown breast.

    © Ian Davies | Macaulay LibraryGeorgia, September 28, 2016
  • Breeding adults

    Very small sandpiper with yellowish legs and thin, slightly drooped bill. Favors muddy habitats.

    © Jay McGowan | Macaulay LibraryNew York, May 01, 2013
    Not all videos have sound
  • Juvenile

    Juveniles have brighter rusty-colored speckling on the back than breeding adults.

    © Alix d'Entremont | Macaulay LibraryNova Scotia, August 27, 2014
  • Breeding adult

    Breeding birds have a heavily streaked throat and breast. Note yellow legs and small size.

    © Jay McGowan | Macaulay LibraryNew York, May 18, 2017
  • Juvenile

    Juveniles have yellow legs, like adults—but note that mud can obscure leg color. Juveniles have brighter rusty-colored speckling on the back than breeding adults.

    © Alix d'Entremont | Macaulay LibraryNova Scotia, August 23, 2014
  • Nonbreeding adult

    Nonbreeding birds have a smudgy throat unlike similar sandpipers. Tends to have a more crouched posture when foraging.

    © Mike Cameron | Macaulay LibraryTexas, April 01, 2017
  • Juvenile

    In flight, note short, dark wings and small overall size.

    © Brooke Miller | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, August 27, 2016
  • Habitat

    Forages on mudflats and the edges of water bodies. Tends to favor muddier shores and estuaries than other sandpipers.

    © Alex Lamoreaux | Macaulay LibraryOhio, July 29, 2016
