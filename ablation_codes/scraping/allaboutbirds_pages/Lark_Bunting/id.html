The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    A heavyset sparrow with a very large, conical bill, and a compact, robust body. The bill and overall shape are reminiscent of a grosbeak or bunting.

    Relative Size

    Larger than a Song Sparrow, slightly smaller than a Fox Sparrow.

    sparrow-sized or smaller
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
      • Length: 5.5-7.1 in (14-18 cm)
      • Weight: 1.3-1.5 oz (35.3-41.3 g)
      • Wingspan: 9.8-11.0 in (25-28 cm)
    © Steve Collins | Macaulay Library
  • Color Pattern

    Breeding males are an unmistakable black with white wing patches. Nonbreeding males, as well as females and immatures, are brownish above, pale with brown streaking below, with extensive white in the upperwing coverts and small white tips to the inner tail feathers. The bill is a distinctive pale blue-gray.

    © Doug Hitchcox | Macaulay Library
  • Behavior

    Lark Buntings forage mostly on or near open ground, hopping or walking like other sparrows as they feed, but they also employ a unique gallop when pursuing fast insect prey, with one foot coming down just before the next. During migration and in winter they form flocks of up to several hundred birds. “Skylarking” males give trilling songs in flight over their territories.

    © Benjamin Clock | Macaulay Library
  • Habitat

    Lark Buntings breed in open grasslands, usually with at least some element of sagebrush, but they also forage and even nest in hayfields and other agricultural fields in some parts of their range. Wintering flocks can be found in many similar habitats from the southern Great Plains into northern Mexico.

    © Chris Wood | Macaulay Library
