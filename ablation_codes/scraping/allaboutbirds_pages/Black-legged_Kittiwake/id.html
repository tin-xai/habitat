The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    A small gull with a plump belly. Kittiwakes look sleek in flight, with rather long, narrow wings, a short, notched tail, and a slim, fairly short bill.

    Relative Size

    Larger than a Bonaparte’s Gull, slightly smaller than a Ring-billed Gull.

    crow-sized
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
      • Length: 15.0-16.1 in (38-41 cm)
      • Weight: 10.8-18.5 oz (305-525 g)
      • Wingspan: 37.0 in (94 cm)
  • Color Pattern

    Breeding adults are pale gray above with neat black wingtips, clean white below, with a yellow bill. The legs and feet are jet black. Nonbreeding adults are similar but acquire a dusky patch behind the ear. Juveniles are quite striking: white overall, with a black M pattern across the upperwing visible in flight. They also have a gray or black collar across the back of the neck, a black tail tip and a black bill.

  • Behavior

    Forages while in flight by dipping or plunging into the sea, almost ternlike, to seize small fish and other prey, usually far offshore.

  • Habitat

    Nests on seaside cliffs, often among auks and Northern Fulmars, in arctic, subarctic, and northern temperate regions. Forages over open ocean, mostly in cold waters.

Regional Differences

Ornithologists recognize two subspecies: the smaller tridactyla of the Arctic and North Atlantic; and the larger pollicaris of the North Pacific, in which adults have more black in the primaries than Atlantic tridactyla.
