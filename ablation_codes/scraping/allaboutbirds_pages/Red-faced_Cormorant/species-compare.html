Pelagic Cormorants are slightly smaller and more slender than Red-faced Cormorants, with a very slender, dark bill.

Double-crested Cormorant

Breeding adult

Double-crested Cormorants are larger and heavier bodied than Red-faced Cormorants, with a thicker bill, larger head, and thicker neck. Double-crested is commonly seen in inland North America, whereas Red-faced occurs only along rocky coasts of the North Pacific.

© Anonymous | Macaulay LibraryWashington, April 11, 2017

Double-crested Cormorant

Breeding adult

Double-crested Cormorants are larger than Red-faced, with a large bill and thick neck usually held bent in flight. Double-crested lacks the white flank patch of breeding Red-faced.

© Evan Lipton | Macaulay LibraryRhode Island, April 28, 2017

Red-faced Cormorant

  • Breeding adult

    Small cormorant with slender bill and red facial skin. Breeding adults have two short crests and square white patches on the flanks.

    © Phillip Edwards | Macaulay LibraryAlaska, June 18, 2016
  • Breeding adult

    Small cormorant with pale bill, blue at base. Breeding adults are glossy purple-green, with short crests on the forecrown and hindcrown.

    © Andrew Spencer | Macaulay LibraryAlaska, May 29, 2013

  • Nests on cliffs, typically alone or in small groups rather than in large colonies. Note extensive red facial skin and pale bill.

    © Timothy Barksdale | Macaulay LibraryAlaska, June 23, 2002
    Not all videos have sound
  • Breeding adult

    Small cormorant of the North Pacific with small, light-colored bill, red facial skin, and fairly thick neck. Breeding adults have white flank patches.

    © Eric VanderWerf | Macaulay LibraryAlaska, June 06, 2015
  • Breeding adult

    Breeding adults have vivid red facial skin, glossy sheen to head, neck, and body, light yellow bill with bluish base, and two short crests.

    © Gerrit Vyn | Macaulay LibraryAlaska, July 26, 2014
  • Immature

    Small cormorant of the North Pacific. Immatures have slender, light-colored bill and lack the bright facial skin and glossy plumage of adults.

    © Ian Davies | Macaulay LibraryHokkaido, December 28, 2013
  • Breeding adult (with Pelagic Cormorants)

    Red-faced Cormorant (center) is slightly larger and more thick-set than Pelagic Cormorants, with brighter facial skin and pale bill.

    © Brian SullivanAlaska, May 18, 2012
  • Breeding adult (with Pelagic Cormorant)

    Red-faced Cormorant (second from left) is slightly larger and more thick-set than Pelagic Cormorants, with brighter facial skin and pale bill.
