The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    A large seabird with a long, thick neck and a smallish bill (larger than Pelagic Cormorant's). It has a long tail, a relatively large head, and large feet. Breeding adults have a short forecrest and hindcrest.

    Relative Size

    Slightly larger than a Pelagic Cormorant, smaller than a Double-crested Cormorant.

    between crow and goose
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
      • Length: 29.1 in (74 cm)
      • Weight: 74.1 oz (2100 g)
      • Wingspan: 46.1 in (117 cm)
    © Peder Svingen | Macaulay Library
  • Color Pattern

    Adult in high breeding condition is glossy blackish, with red facial skin, a yellowish bill with a pale blue base, and white patches on the flanks. Nonbreeding adults lack the white flank patches and have less glossy plumage and less red facial skin. Juvenile is uniformly dark brown but still shows a strongly contrasting, pale bill.

    © Phillip Edwards | Macaulay Library
  • Behavior

    Forages by diving to pursue fish and other marine organisms, which the birds extract from crevices in the seafloor or capture while swimming. Male and female perform intricate displays at the nest site, involving rapid movements of wings, heads, necks, bills, and feet.

    © Jeff Bleam | Macaulay Library
  • Habitat

    Nests on remote, steep cliffs on Alaskan islands and forages in adjacent inshore (littoral) waters. Disperses to sea after nesting but generally forages within sight of land.

    © Robert Raker | Macaulay Library
