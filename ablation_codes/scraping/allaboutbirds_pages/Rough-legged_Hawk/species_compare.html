Red-tailed Hawk

Adult (borealis)

Adult Red-tailed Hawks have a "belly band" of dark streaks and a tail that is not banded. Rough-legged Hawks have either a heavily streaked breast (males) or a large dark belly patch (females), and a white-based tail with a dark band at the tip.

Red-tailed Hawk

Adult (borealis)

Red-tailed Hawks have broader wings and lack the large, dark patches at the wrist seen on Rough-legged Hawks. Red-tails usually show a band of dark streaks on the belly and dark patches between the shoulder and wrist (patagium).

Red-tailed Hawks have broader wings and thicker bodies than Rough-legged Hawks. Juvenile Red-tails have narrowly banded tails but lack the white tail base and dark tail tip of light-morph Rough-legged Hawks.

Ferruginous Hawk

Light morph

Ferruginous Hawks are larger-bodied than Rough-legged Hawks, with straight wings that taper toward the tips. They are paler below than Rough-legged Hawks, without the large, dark wrist patches, and without heavy markings on the breast or belly.
