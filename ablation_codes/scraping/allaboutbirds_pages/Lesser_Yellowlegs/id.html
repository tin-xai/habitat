The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    A slender and elegant, medium-sized shorebirds with long legs and a long neck. The bill is straight and thin, only slightly longer than the bird's head (shorter and straighter than Greater Yellowlegs' bill).

    Relative Size

    Larger than a Dunlin, smaller than a Greater Yellowlegs.

    between robin and crow
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
      • Length: 9.1-10.6 in (23-27 cm)
      • Weight: 2.8-3.2 oz (79.5-90.9 g)
      • Wingspan: 23.2-25.2 in (59-64 cm)
    © Fernando Farias | Macaulay Library
  • Color Pattern

    Breeding Lesser Yellowlegs are grayish brown birds with vivid yellow legs. They have fine, gray streaking across the head and neck, a white eyering, and white spots on the back and wings. Nonbreeding birds are more subdued gray-brown with less streaking and spotting. Bill is entirely dark.

    © Brendan Klick | Macaulay Library
  • Behavior

    The Lesser Yellowlegs has a graceful, high-stepping gait as it moves deftly across mudflats and marshes. Flight is buoyant and relaxed, with legs extending beyond the tail.

    © Matthew D. Medler | Macaulay Library
  • Habitat

    Wetland habitats ranging from tidal flats to sewage ponds to flooded fields; often in the company of other shorebird species. Breeds in open forests and meadows interspersed with marshes and bogs.

    © Daniel Jauvin | Macaulay Library
