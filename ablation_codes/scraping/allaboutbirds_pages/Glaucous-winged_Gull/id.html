The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    A large, heavyset gull with a big, somewhat flat head and a long, heavy bill with a thick tip and pronounced angle along the lower edge.

    Relative Size

    Larger than an Iceland Gull, smaller than a Glaucous Gull.

    between crow and goose
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
      • Length: 19.7-23.2 in (50-59 cm)
      • Weight: 31.8-42.3 oz (900-1200 g)
      • Wingspan: 47.2-56.3 in (120-143 cm)
  • Color Pattern

    Breeding adults are pearly gray above, including the wingtips, and white below. The wingtips have white spots. The bill is yellow and the legs and feet pinkish. Nonbreeding adults have some brownish mottling or streaking on the head. Juveniles are pale brown overall, including wingtips. Subadult plumages are intermediate between juvenile and adult.

  • Behavior

    Walks, swims, and flies in coastal areas and hunts prey visually, pulling fish, crabs, mollusks, and sea stars from rocks or pools, often dropping hard prey from the air to break it open on rocks. Sometimes joins large flocks of seabirds to feed on concentrated schools of small fish. Also scavenges around fishing boats, fish-processing plants, eagles and bears, and visits landfills. Often rests and forages in flocks of several gull species, especially during the nonbreeding season.

  • Habitat

    Nests on small treeless islands in the ocean but near the mainland. Sometimes nests on buildings and other structures. Forages along marine shorelines, both rocky and sandy, in rivermouths, bays, and estuaries, and in landfills.
