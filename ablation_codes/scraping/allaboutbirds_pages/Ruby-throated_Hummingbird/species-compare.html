Black-chinned Hummingbird

Adult male

Adult male Black-chinned Hummingbirds look similar to adult male Ruby-throated Hummingbirds, especially in poor light, but Black-chinned Hummingbirds have a purple throat and Ruby-throateds have a red throat.

© Ian Hearn | Macaulay LibraryIdaho, July 13, 2017

Black-chinned Hummingbird

Female/immature

Where their ranges overlap, look carefully at the tail on perched birds. Black-chinned Hummingbirds have longer wings that reach the tip of the tail or go slightly beyond it, whereas Ruby-throated Hummingbirds have a longer tail, extending well beyond the wingtips.

© Paul Fenwick | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, September 14, 2016
View Full Species Account
Similar Species

Broad-tailed Hummingbird

Adult male

The breeding range of Broad-tailed Hummingbirds does not overlap with Ruby-throated Hummingbirds. Adult male Broad-tailed Hummingbirds lack the adult male Ruby-throated Hummingbird's dark eye patch.

© Gregg Friesen | Macaulay LibraryColorado, July 29, 2017

Broad-tailed Hummingbird

Female/immature

Broad-tailed Hummingbirds have cinnamon-colored tail feathers, whereas the tail feathers on Ruby-throated Hummingbirds are green and black. Note range differences.

© Bill Maynard | Macaulay LibraryColorado, August 28, 2016

Ruby-throated Hummingbird

  • Adult male

    Tiny, with long wings and bill. Dusky below with a bright red throat, black mask, and green crown.

    © Brian Kulvete | Macaulay LibraryConnecticut, May 03, 2018
  • Female

    © German Garcia | Macaulay LibraryMorelos, September 14, 2020
  • Adult male

    Tiny bird with emerald green back and brilliant red throat. The throat is iridescent and looks black or dark red, then lights up when viewed at the correct angle.

    © Timothy Barksdale | Macaulay LibraryNew York, May 01, 2004
    Not all videos have sound
  • Adult male

    © Jeremy Cohen | Macaulay LibraryWisconsin, August 03, 2020
  • Adult male

    In some lights the throat of the adult male can appear black.

    © Grace C | Macaulay LibraryVermont, May 16, 2020
  • Female

    Hovers with pinpoint precision, beating wings so fast they're almost invisible. Drinks nectar by probing flowers with long, thin bill.

    © Timothy Barksdale | Macaulay LibraryMissouri, January 01, 1997
    Not all videos have sound
  • Female

    Feeds on a variety of flowering plants. Tiny, with long wings and bill. Mostly white below, with buffy flanks, a dusky mask, and green upperparts.

    © bellemare celine | Macaulay LibraryQuebec, August 21, 2019
  • Adult male

    Upperparts bright metallic green, and short tail is black and forked.

    © Joey Herron | Macaulay LibraryWest Virginia, June 29, 2017
  • Female

    Eats insects in addition to nectar, often plucking them from spiderwebs or taking them from a sapsucker's sap wells.

    © Eric Liner | Macaulay LibraryNew York, June 10, 2006
    Not all videos have sound
  • Female

    Upperparts bright metallic green, and short tail is mostly black with white tips to the outer feathers.

    © Warren Lynn | Macaulay LibraryIndiana, July 15, 2015
  • Immature male

    Immature males have some red feathers on the throat.

    © Dina Perry | Macaulay LibraryTexas, September 11, 2018
  • Nest

    Nestlings have shorter bills than adults. Nest is thimble-sized, made with spiderweb that stretches as the young birds grow.

    © Timothy Barksdale | Macaulay LibraryMissouri, June 01, 1999
    Not all videos have sound
  • Juvenile

    © Cam Nikkel | Macaulay LibraryManitoba, September 05, 2018
  • Adult male

    Sometimes perches prominently on exposed branches.

    © Paul Bigelow | Macaulay LibraryNew York, May 19, 2017
