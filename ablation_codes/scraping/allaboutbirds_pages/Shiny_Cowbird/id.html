The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    A stout songbird with a short, thick-based, finely pointed bill and moderately long wings and tail.

    Relative Size

    Slightly larger than an Eastern Bluebird, smaller than a Red-winged Blackbird.

    robin-sized
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
    © Steven Mlodinow | Macaulay Library
  • Color Pattern

    Adult males appear blackish from a distance; in good light they are velvety purple. Adult females are brown overall with a pale eyebrow and throat. Juveniles are also brownish but heavily streaked below, similar to juvenile Brown-headed Cowbirds.

    © Mark Davidson | Macaulay Library
  • Behavior

    Shiny Cowbirds forage in flocks, often with other blackbird species. They feed mostly on the ground, searching for seeds, grains, and insects. Males sing a gurgling song and display to females in the air, from a tree, or on the ground.

    © Timothy Barksdale | Macaulay Library
  • Habitat

    Forages in many open and semiopen habitats, from parks and gardens, roadsides, and farm fields to the edges of riparian woodlands and mangrove forests.

    © Matías Garrido | Macaulay Library

Regional Differences

Ornithologists recognize at least seven subspecies in the Caribbean and South America, all rather similar to one another. Only the smallest subspecies, minimus, is known in the United States.
