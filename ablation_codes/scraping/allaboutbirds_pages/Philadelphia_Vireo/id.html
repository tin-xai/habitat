Philadelphia Vireo Photos and Videos

Somewhat stocky songbird with a thick, hooked bill. Adults in fresh plumage are bright yellow below with a yellow throat. The dark gray crown contrasts with the white eyebrow, though this can be indistinct on some birds.

Small and stocky songbird. Rather plain and unmarked overall with a yellowish belly, gray crown, and grayish brown back. Note unmarked wings. Some individuals are brighter than others.

The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    A small songbird; small for a vireo but still chunkier than a warbler. The bill is small compared with other vireos.

    Relative Size

    Larger than a Ruby-crowned Kinglet, slightly smaller than a Red-eyed Vireo.

    sparrow-sized or smaller
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
      • Length: 4.2-4.9 in (10.7-12.5 cm)
      • Weight: 0.4-0.6 oz (10.3-16.1 g)
      • Wingspan: 7.5-8.3 in (19-21 cm)
    © Luke Seitz | Macaulay Library
  • Color Pattern

    Olive above and pale below, often with a yellow throat and underparts. Dark grayish crown, with a dark line all the way through the eye and a pale stripe over the eye.

    © Ian Davies | Macaulay Library
  • Behavior

    Philadelphia Vireos forage relatively high in the trees, slowly moving along branches and watching leaves for insect prey, especially caterpillars and beetles during the breeding season. Migrants and wintering birds often join mixed-species flocks of woodland birds.

    © Timothy Barksdale | Macaulay Library
  • Habitat

    Breeds in regrowing deciduous and mixed woodlands, especially those with aspen, birch, alder, cherry, ash, black spruce, and balsam fir, as well as willow and alder thickets. Migrating and wintering birds show up in many kinds of wooded habitats, including mature forest edge.

    © Jay McGowan | Macaulay Library
