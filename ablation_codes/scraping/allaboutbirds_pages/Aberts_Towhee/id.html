The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    A large sparrow with a short, conical bill, an elongated body for a sparrow, and a rather long tail. The overall shape is similar to Northern Cardinal (but with a smaller bill and without the crest).

    Relative Size

    Slightly larger than a Northern Cardinal, smaller than a Woodhouse’s Scrub-Jay.

    robin-sized
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
      • Length: 8.3-9.1 in (21.2-23.1 cm)
      • Weight: 1.5-1.9 oz (42.5-54.9 g)
      • Wingspan: 10.4-11.6 in (26.5-29.5 cm)
    © Jeffrey Moore | Macaulay Library
  • Color Pattern

    Abert’s Towhee is grayish-brown above, pinkish brown below, with rusty undertail coverts and black plumage bordering a pale grayish bill.

    © Paul Suchanek | Macaulay Library
  • Behavior

    Abert’s Towhees spend most of their time on the ground. They forage mostly beneath dense undergrowth, flying very little during the course of a day, and seldom perch high in trees. Even singing males tend to prefer brush or the lower parts of trees.

    © Gregory Griffith | Macaulay Library
  • Habitat

    Low, dense cover along desert streams and riverbeds with cottonwoods, willows, or mesquite.

    © Keith D Kamper | Macaulay Library
