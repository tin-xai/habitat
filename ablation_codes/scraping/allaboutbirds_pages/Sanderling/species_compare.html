Least Sandpiper

Nonbreeding adult

Least Sandpipers are smaller than Sanderlings with a dingy breast and yellow legs. Sanderlings have a clean breast and dark legs.

© Ian Davies | Macaulay LibraryGeorgia, September 28, 2016
View Full Species Account
Similar Species

Semipalmated Sandpiper

Nonbreeding adult

Semipalmated Sandpipers are smaller than Sanderlings with fine streaking on the breast that nonbreeding Sanderlings don't have.

© Jonathan Eckerson | Macaulay LibraryMassachusetts, July 16, 2017
View Full Species Account
Similar Species

Western Sandpiper

Nonbreeding adult

Western Sandpipers are smaller than Sanderlings. The head and back is finely streaked on Western Sandpipers, but unstreaked and pale gray on nonbreeding Sanderlings.

© Patrick Maurice | Macaulay LibraryFlorida, November 06, 2016
View Full Species Account
Similar Species

White-rumped Sandpiper

Molting adult

White-rumped Sandpipers have streaking on the breast and flanks while nonbreeding Sanderlings have a clean breast and flanks.

© Dorian Anderson | Macaulay LibraryMassachusetts, September 11, 2011
View Full Species Account
Similar Species

Red Knot

Breeding adult

Red Knots are larger than Sanderlings with a stouter bill and more extensive rusty coloring on the belly.

© Ian Davies | Macaulay LibraryAlaska, May 25, 2013
View Full Species Account
Similar Species

Red Knot

Nonbreeding

Red Knots are larger than Sanderlings with a stouter bill. Nonbreeding knots have barring on the flanks while nonbreeding Sanderlings have a clean white belly.

© David Clark | Macaulay LibraryVirginia, October 26, 2008
View Full Species Account
Similar Species

Dunlin

Nonbreeding adult

Dunlin are larger than Sanderlings, with a longer and often drooping bill.

© Alix d'Entremont | Macaulay LibraryNova Scotia, October 12, 2014
View Full Species Account
Similar Species

Snowy Plover

Nonbreeding

Snowy Plovers are smaller with shorter bills than Sanderlings. They are usually found higher on the beach and don’t chase after waves like Sanderlings do.

© Brian Sullivan | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, January 12, 2013
View Full Species Account
Similar Species

Piping Plover

Juvenile

Juvenile and nonbreeding Piping Plovers have a smaller, stubbier bill than Sanderlings. They also have yellow (not black) legs.

© Sue Barth | Macaulay LibraryNew York, August 15, 2016

Main Species

Sanderling

  • Breeding adult

    Plump sandpiper with a stout black bill. Breeding birds have rusty mottling on the back, head, and breast. Note unmarked sides and black legs.

    © Jonathan Lugo | Macaulay LibraryVirginia, May 26, 2016
  • Nonbreeding adult

    Plump sandpiper with a stout black bill. Nonbreeding birds are pale gray above and white below without streaking. Darker shoulder bar sometimes visible.

    © Ryan Schain | Macaulay LibraryMassachusetts, November 16, 2014
  • Juveniles

    Forages at the water's edge, typically on sandy beaches during winter; often found inland during migration.

    © DAVID BROWN | Macaulay LibraryNew York, October 01, 2004
    Not all videos have sound
  • Juvenile

    Juveniles have a checkered back and unmarked white underparts.

    © Jonathan Eckerson | Macaulay LibraryMassachusetts, September 02, 2016
  • Nonbreeding adult

    In flight, note dark leading edge of the wing from above and all white wings from below.

    © John P Richardson | Macaulay LibraryMinnesota, May 02, 2017
  • Breeding adult

    Breeding adults have varying amounts of rusty wash on the upperparts. Note stout black bill and clean white underparts.

    © Jay McGowan | Macaulay LibraryNew York, June 01, 2015
  • Breeding adult

    Plump shorebird with a stout black bill and black legs. Breeding birds have rusty mottled upperparts.

    © Alex Eberts | Macaulay LibraryOhio, May 27, 2017
  • Juvenile

    Juveniles have speckled backs and unmarked white underparts.

    © Jay McGowan | Macaulay LibraryNew York, September 10, 2014
  • Nonbreeding adult

    Scurries back and forth chasing waves while foraging in loose groups.

    © Theadora Block | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, January 29, 2017
  • Flock

    Forms large flocks during migration. Found on sandy beaches chasing waves.

    © Tom Unsicker | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, August 07, 2016
