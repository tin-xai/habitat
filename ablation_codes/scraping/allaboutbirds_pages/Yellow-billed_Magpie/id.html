The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    A very large songbird with a heavy body, a large head, and an extremely long tail. The bill is stout and slightly curved. The wings are wide and rounded.

    Relative Size

    Much larger than a California Scrub-Jay, slightly smaller than an American Crow.

    between robin and crow
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
      • Length: 16.9-21.3 in (43-54 cm)
      • Weight: 5.3-6.0 oz (150-170 g)
      • Wingspan: 24.0 in (61 cm)
  • Color Pattern

    A black-and-white bird with iridescent blue in the wings and a bright yellow bill. When perched, the shoulders and belly are stark white against the black body. In flight, the primary feathers (in the outer wing) show extensive white as well.

  • Behavior

    Walks slowly on the ground looking for grain, insects, and rodents. Flies with deep, fairly slow wingbeats. A social bird that is usually in small flocks in summer and in larger flocks during the nonbreeding season.

  • Habitat

    Nests in tall trees in oak savanna or similar habitats of central California. Forages in grassland, pasture, cultivated fields, and orchards, sometimes in trees.
