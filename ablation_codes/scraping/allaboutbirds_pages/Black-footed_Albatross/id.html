The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    A very large seabird with long, narrow wings, a heavy head and neck, and a long, hooked bill.

    Relative Size

    Much larger than a Western Gull, slightly smaller than a Short-tailed Albatross

    goose-sized or larger
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
      • Length: 25.2-29.1 in (64-74 cm)
      • Weight: 77.6-151.7 oz (2200-4300 g)
      • Wingspan: 76.0-85.0 in (193-216 cm)
    © Brian Sullivan | Macaulay Library
  • Color Pattern

    Dark brown overall, sometimes with whitish plumage in uppertail and undertail coverts or on the face. Feet and bill blackish.

    © Blake Matheson | Macaulay Library
  • Behavior

    Albatrosses have a very distinctive flight style known as dynamic soaring. They rarely flap their wings except in very light winds. They typically fly low to the water, sometimes down in the wave troughs, and then swoop up into the sky, catch the wind, and accelerate downwind before swooping low to the water again. Often forages in groups, sometimes mixed with other seabirds and marine mammals.

    © Benjamin Clock | Macaulay Library
  • Habitat

    Nests on low, sandy islands in the tropical North Pacific. Forages both near to shore (though usually not within sight of land) and far offshore, in places where upwelling or converging currents concentrate nutrients and prey at the sea surface.

    © Brian Sullivan
