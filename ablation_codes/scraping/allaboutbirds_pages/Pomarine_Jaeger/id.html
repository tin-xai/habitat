The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    Bulky gull-like seabird; the largest and most barrel chested of the three jaegers. At all ages, note broad wings. Breeding adults are unique with elongated spoon-shaped central tail feathers. Bill is also thicker than other jaegers.

    Relative Size

    Larger than a Parasitic Jaeger; smaller than a Herring Gull.

    between crow and goose
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
      • Length: 16.5-24.0 in (42-61 cm)
      • Weight: 18.4-30.1 oz (521-852 g)
      • Wingspan: 45.3-52.0 in (115-132 cm)
    © Ian Davies | Macaulay Library
  • Color Pattern

    Note blackish-brown upperparts, messy black cap that extends below the bill, and extensive white flash on upper and underside of outer wing. Rare dark morph adult is entirely blackish-brown but still has white wing flashes. Immature birds are variable, but in general they are barred with brown above and have bold black-and-white bars on the underwing and undertail coverts. Typically shows white shafts on 6 to 8 outer wing feathers (more than other jaegers) and more obvious white flash on underwing.

    © Brian Sullivan
  • Behavior

    All jaegers chase other birds and steal their food. Pomarine Jaegers have a powerful, direct flight style and are less acrobatic than the smaller jaegers.

    © Timothy Barksdale | Macaulay Library
  • Habitat

    Breeds on Arctic tundra, spends the rest of the year at sea.

    © Luke Seitz
