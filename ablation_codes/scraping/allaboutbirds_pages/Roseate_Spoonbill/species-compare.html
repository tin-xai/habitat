Roseate Spoonbill

  • Adult

    Large, pink wading bird with a long, spoon-shaped bill. Adults have brighter pink shoulders and a bare yellowish green head.

    © Peter Hawrylyshyn | Macaulay LibraryFlorida, March 07, 2015
  • Juvenile

    Juveniles look similar to adults, but are paler pink and have a completely feathered head. Spoon-shaped bill is distinctive.

    © James Rieman | Macaulay LibraryTexas, July 25, 2017
  • Adult

    Roseate Spoonbills forage in shallow waters for aquatic invertebrates sweeping their head side to side with the bill partly opened.

    © Benjamin Clock | Macaulay LibraryLouisiana, June 02, 2010
    Not all videos have sound
  • Juvenile

    Flies with its neck outstretched. Note pink underwings.

    © Neil Rucker | Macaulay LibraryArizona, October 04, 2020
  • Adult

    Flies with deep and strong wingbeats.

    © Darren Clark | Macaulay LibraryTexas, April 12, 2016
  • Adult

    Roseate Spoonbills roost and nest in colonies with other waterbirds in trees and shrubs at the edges of wetlands and estuaries.

    © Larry Arbanas | Macaulay LibraryTexas, April 27, 2007
    Not all videos have sound
  • Adult

    © Brent Cox | Macaulay LibraryTexas, December 23, 2020
  • Adult

    Nests and roosts in trees and shrubs including mangrove, sea myrtle, hackberry, and mesquite.

    © Darren Clark | Macaulay LibraryTexas, April 12, 2016
  • Adult and juvenile

    Roseate Spoonbills build bulky stick nests in trees and shrubs that often hang over standing water. They typically raise 3 to 4 chicks every year.

    © Larry Arbanas | Macaulay LibraryTexas, April 27, 2007
    Not all videos have sound
  • Juvenile

    Juveniles are pale pink with a completely feathered head.

    © Alyssa Suder | Macaulay LibraryNew Jersey, July 22, 2017
  • Habitat

    Found in freshwater and saltwater wetlands, mangroves, forested swamps, rivers, bays, estuaries, mudflats, and lagoons.

    © David Hall | Macaulay LibraryFlorida, August 29, 2017
  • Adult

    Flies with its neck stretched out with powerful wingbeats.

    © Larry Arbanas | Macaulay LibraryTexas, April 26, 2007
