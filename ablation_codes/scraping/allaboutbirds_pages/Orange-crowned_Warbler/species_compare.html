Orange-crowned Warbler

  • Adult/immature

    Small warbler with a thin, pointy bill. Generally yellow-olive overall with a faint eyeline. Birds in the West are brighter than birds in the East, but the undertail coverts are always yellow.

    © Ian Davies | Macaulay LibraryArizona, October 21, 2017
  • Immature

    Immature birds often grayer than adults. Head color varies regionally from gray in the East to yellow-green in the West, but they all have yellow undertail coverts (not visible in this photo). Note broken eyering and faint eyeline.

    © Ryan Schain | Macaulay LibraryMassachusetts, November 03, 2010
  • Adult

    Forages for insects on the ground and in trees. Pokes through leaf litter, and probes bark and moss with the sharp, fine-tipped bill.

    © Eric Liner | Macaulay LibraryAlaska, July 01, 2007
    Not all videos have sound
  • Adult

    Western birds are brighter yellow-green with faint blurry streaking on the breast. Note thin, pointy bill and yellow undertail coverts.

    © Jon Isacoff | Macaulay LibraryWashington, March 10, 2014
  • Adult

    Generally yellow-olive overall, but head color varies regionally from gray in the East to yellow-green in the West. Note broken eyering bisected by a faint eyeline.

    © Blair Dudeck | Macaulay LibraryBritish Columbia, April 26, 2020
  • Adult

    Flits rapidly through shrubs, trees, vine tangles, and other vegetation as it glean insects from leaves and buds.

    © Timothy Barksdale | Macaulay LibraryAlaska, June 01, 2002
    Not all videos have sound
  • Adult

    Adults have an orangish crown patch that is usually concealed and difficult to see.

    © Brian Sullivan | Macaulay LibraryAlaska, June 30, 2019
  • Adult/immature

    Gray-headed subspecies has a grayer head, but note faint eyeline, thin, pointy bill, and yellow undertail coverts.

    © Paul Fenwick | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, September 20, 2016
  • Adult

    Orange-crowned Warblers supplement their diet with fruit and nectar during the winter. Sometimes visits fruit, suet, and hummingbird feeders.

    © Timothy Barksdale | Macaulay LibraryTexas, March 03, 1997
    Not all videos have sound
  • Immature

    Dull yellow-olive overall with a thin, pointy bill. Head color varies regionally from gray in the East to yellow-green in the West, but they all have yellow undertail coverts.

    © Ryan Schain | Macaulay LibraryMassachusetts, November 06, 2016
  • Adult

    Populations on the Channel Islands and nearby mainland tend to have streaky breasts and undertail coverts. Note thin, pointy bill.

    © Nigel Voaden | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, May 04, 2015
  • Adult

    Sometimes visits suet feeders, especially during the winter.

    © Gregory Griffith | Macaulay LibraryTexas, January 01, 1994
    Not all videos have sound
  • Habitat

    Often found foraging in dense shrubbery and low trees. Note thin, pointy bill and yellow undertail coverts.

    © Jeremiah Trimble | Macaulay LibraryMassachusetts, December 17, 2016
