The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    A stocky, football-shaped seabird with a very short neck, a large head, and an enormous bill (especially during the breeding season) that is tall and laterally flattened.

    Relative Size

    Larger than a Pigeon Guillemot, smaller than a Common Murre.

    crow-sized
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
    © Kasia & Takashi Someya | Macaulay Library
  • Color Pattern

    Breeding adults are blackish with a white face and a yellow bill with a red-orange tip. They have a white breast and belly, and vivid orange feet. Dark, fleshy “horns” extend above the eyes. Nonbreeding adults have sooty gray faces and lose the yellow portion of the bill revealing a smaller, grayish base. Immatures have smaller, all-gray bills compared to adults.

    © Andrew Spencer | Macaulay Library
  • Behavior

    Forages by diving and swimming underwater, “flying” with the wings to chase prey, which it catches in the bill and either eats underwater or carries back in the bill to feed young. Near the breeding grounds, often gathers in flocks with many other seabird species. Male and females perform attractive courtship displays with bobbing heads and billing.

    © Timothy Barksdale | Macaulay Library
  • Habitat

    Nests on rocky seaside cliffs in Siberia, Alaska, and northern British Columbia. Forages in littoral (near-shore) ocean areas when nesting but for most of the year lives and forages only in deep pelagic waters of the central North Pacific, very far from land.

    © Nigel Voaden | Macaulay Library
