White-breasted Nuthatch

Adult

White-breasted Nuthatches are larger with a black hood and a white face, including white all the way around the eye, instead of the Pygmy Nuthatch’s crisp brown cap.

© Ryan Schain | Macaulay LibraryMassachusetts, February 22, 2011

Red-breasted Nuthatch

Adult

Red-breasted Nuthatches have a prominent white stripe over the eye and reddish-cinnamon underparts, two features Pygmy Nuthatches don't have.

© Scott Martin | Macaulay LibraryMassachusetts, February 03, 2017

Brown-headed Nuthatch

Adult/immature

The Brown-headed Nuthatch of the southeastern U.S. looks very similar to the Pygmy Nuthatch, but their ranges don’t overlap.

© Shelley Rutkin | Macaulay LibraryNorth Carolina, March 17, 2017

Bushtit

Male (Pacific)

Bushtits have much longer tails than the stubby-tailed Pygmy Nuthatch. Bushtits don't climb up and down tree trunks the way Pygmy Nuthatches do.

© Caroline Lambert | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, January 28, 2016

Main Species

Pygmy Nuthatch

  • Adult

    Tiny songbird with a short, square tail, a large head, and a straight, sharp bill. Slate gray wings and back; rich brown cap ending in a sharp line through the eye. Underparts are whitish to pale buff.

    © Bryan Calk | Macaulay LibraryNew Mexico, August 03, 2020
  • Adult

    © Ian Routley | Macaulay LibraryBritish Columbia, April 21, 2018
  • Adults/immatures

    Often travels and breeds in extended family groups. Call is a squeaky, nasal note like the sound of a rubber ducky.

    © Larry Arbanas | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, January 01, 2005
    Not all videos have sound
  • Adult

    The smallest nuthatch. It has a brown crown, blue-gray back, and buffy to grayish underparts. Although not visible in this photo, they also have a pale spot on the back of the neck.

    © Blair Dudeck | Macaulay LibraryBritish Columbia, June 05, 2020
  • Adult

    © Marky Mutchler | Macaulay LibraryNew Mexico, August 03, 2020
  • Adult

    © M Nagy | Macaulay LibraryNew Mexico, January 19, 2020
  • Adult

    © Will Sebern | Macaulay LibraryMontana, January 01, 2021
  • Juvenile

    Juveniles are paler overall with a yellowish bill and grayish crown.

    © Jeff Bleam | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, July 18, 2017
  • Habitat

    Found in pine forests in western North America, especially mature ponderosa pine forests.

    © Graham Gerdeman | Macaulay LibraryNevada, September 20, 2016
