Main Species

Tundra Swan

  • Adult (Whistling)

    Very large and entirely white with a long neck, with black bill showing a small yellow spot on the black facial skin in front of the eye.

    © Ian Davies | Macaulay LibraryAlaska, July 12, 2013
  • Adult (Bewick's)

    Very large and entirely white with a long neck, with black bill showing yellow at the base, and black legs. "Bewick's" is the Eurasian form.

    © Ian Davies | Macaulay LibraryIshikawa, December 19, 2013
  • Adult (Whistling)

    Yellow at base of bill is often hard to see and sometimes is absent. Note curved, not pointed, line above the eyes where bill meets forehead.

    © Jay McGowan | Macaulay LibraryNew York, March 07, 2015
    Not all videos have sound
  • Juvenile (Whistling)

    Older juveniles are pale dusky-brown with extensive white highlights, and a mostly pink bill with black tip and base.

    © Doug Hitchcox | Macaulay LibraryMaine, November 11, 2020
  • Adult (Whistling)

    Flies with neck outstretched. Entirely white with black legs and variable amount of yellow on the base of black bill.

    © Darlene Friedman | Macaulay LibraryMichigan, March 25, 2019
  • Adult (Bewick's)

    © Otto Samwald | Macaulay LibraryCentral Macedonia, February 11, 2016
  • Juvenile (Whistling)

    Young juveniles are fluffy, entirely grayish white, with a pale bill.

    © Alvan Buckley | Macaulay LibraryNorthwest Territories, July 10, 2013
  • Adult (Whistling)

    Some birds have very little or no yellow at the base of the bill. Extent of facial skin connecting bill to eye variable, but usually not extensive.

    © Jody Wells | Macaulay LibraryBritish Columbia, February 06, 2017
  • Adult (Bewick's)

    "Bewick's," the Eurasian form, has extensive yellow at the base of the bill.

    © Thomas Gibson | Macaulay LibraryEngland, November 19, 2016
  • Adult (Whistling)

    Flies with neck outstretched. Entirely white with black legs and variable amount of yellow on the base of black bill.

    © Steven Mlodinow | Macaulay LibraryColorado, January 31, 2017
  • Habitat

    Often occurs in flocks on ponds, rivers, lakes, estuaries, and bays.

    © Karol Pasquinelli | Macaulay LibraryPennsylvania, March 12, 2017
