Black-necked Stilt

Adult (Black-necked)

Black-necked Stilts are shorter with a more dainty appearance than American Avocets. They also have a shorter and straighter bill than the longer and upturned bill on American Avocets.

© Dorian Anderson | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, January 21, 2017

Marbled Godwit

Breeding adult

Marbled Godwits are browner overall than American Avocets with a heavily barred back and belly (during the breeding season). Their bill is also bi-colored but the bill on American Avocets is solid black .

© Jeff Timmons | Macaulay LibraryFlorida, June 22, 2017

American Avocet

  • Breeding adult

    Long-legged shorebird with a long, upturned bill. Breeding birds have a rusty head and neck and a black-and-white body.

    © Ian Davies | Macaulay LibraryMontana, June 01, 2020
  • Nonbreeding adult/immature

    Tall shorebird with an upturned bill. Nonbreeding birds have a grayish white head.

    © Denny Swaby | Macaulay LibraryBodden Town, March 14, 2020
  • Breeding adults

    Forages in shallow wetlands. Sweeps its bill side to side through the water to catch invertebrates.

    © Timothy Barksdale | Macaulay LibraryMissouri, May 01, 1997
    Not all videos have sound
  • Nonbreeding/immature

    In flight, note black patch on the back.

    © Darren Clark | Macaulay LibraryIdaho, October 05, 2018
  • Breeding adult

    Swings its long upturned bill through shallow water to catch small invertebrates.

    © Paul Barchilon | Macaulay LibraryColorado, May 10, 2017
  • Breeding adult

    Slowly walks through wetlands shaking its foot with each step.

    © Larry Arbanas | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, May 01, 2005
    Not all videos have sound
  • Breeding adult

    Tall and graceful shorebird with a striking black-and-white body, a rusty head and neck, and an upturned bill. Females tend to have more sharply curved bills than males.

    © Peter Nichols | Macaulay LibraryMinnesota, April 15, 2017
  • Juvenile

    Young leave the nest within 24 hours. Juveniles are downy with a mottled back, gaining adultlike plumage as they age. Juveniles have a straighter bill than adults.

    © Susan Teefy | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, July 04, 2017
  • Nonbreeding adults

    Often rests on one leg, tucking its bill under the shoulder.

    © Jay McGowan | Macaulay LibraryNew York, September 23, 2012
    Not all videos have sound
  • Breeding adult

    Often wades while foraging but also swims. Males tend to have less curve in the bill than females.

    © Tim Lenz | Macaulay LibraryColorado, May 11, 2016
  • Breeding adult

    Found in shallow fresh and saltwater wetlands.

    © Ad Konings | Macaulay LibraryTexas, May 02, 2020
  • Breeding adults

    Swipes bill from side to side or pecks into the water to catch aquatic invertebrates and small fish.

    © Benjamin Clock | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, March 24, 2013
