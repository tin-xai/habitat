Black-capped Chickadee

Adult

Black-capped Chickadees lack the Mountain Chickadee's white stripe over the eye. Black-capped tends to be seen among deciduous trees along drainages, while Mountain Chickadees are more often found in evergreen forests.

© Scott Martin | Macaulay LibraryMassachusetts, February 03, 2017

Similar Species

Bridled Titmouse

Adult

Bridled Titmouse has a pointed crest to the head, unlike Mountain Chickadee's rounded head. The Bridled Titmouse's stripe over the eye extends down the neck, unlike Mountain Chickadee's.

© Matt Davis | Macaulay LibraryArizona, March 09, 2016

Boreal Chickadee

Adult

Boreal Chickadees have limited overlap with Mountain Chickadees, mainly in northwestern Canada. Boreal Chickadee has a brown head and no white stripe over the eye.

© Chris Peters | Macaulay LibraryNova Scotia, January 02, 2017

Chestnut-backed Chickadee

Adult

Chestnut-backed Chickadee lacks a white stripe over the eye and has a rich brown back, unlike Mountain Chickadee's gray back.

© Paul Fenwick | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, September 04, 2016

Carolina Chickadee

Adult

Carolina Chickadees don't overlap in range with Mountain Chickadees. They also lack Mountain's white stripe over the eye.

© Michael O'Brien | Macaulay LibraryNew Jersey, February 18, 2017

Main Species

Mountain Chickadee

  • Adult

    Tiny, large-headed but small-billed, with a long, narrow tail and a round belly. Grayish overall with white cheeks, a black cap and throat, and a white eyebrow.

    © Michael Woodruff | Macaulay LibraryWashington, April 22, 2019
  • Adult

    Small and inquisitive. Looks similar to other chickadees, but white eyebrow stripe separates them from other chickadees.

    © Marc North | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, December 07, 2015
  • Adult

    Small, active bird with black bib, white cheeks, and white eyebrow stripe.

    © Timothy Barksdale | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, July 29, 1997
    Not all videos have sound
  • Adult

    Nests in holes in trees created by woodpeckers or excavates its own in trees with soft wood. Also uses nest boxes.

    © John C Breitsch | Macaulay LibraryColorado, May 28, 2017
  • Adult (with Downy Woodpecker)

    Visits bird feeders year-round to eat suet, black oil sunflower seeds, and nyjer seeds.

    © Dennis Leonard | Macaulay LibraryBritish Columbia, March 29, 2017
  • Habitat

    Active and acrobatic, clinging to small limbs and twigs or hanging upside down from pine cones. Found in evergreen forests in mountains of western North America.

    © Ian Hearn | Macaulay LibraryIdaho, July 25, 2017
