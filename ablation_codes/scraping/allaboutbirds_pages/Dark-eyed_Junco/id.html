The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    The Dark-eyed Junco is a medium-sized sparrow with a rounded head, a short, stout bill and a fairly long, conspicuous tail.

    Relative Size

    Slightly larger than a Chipping Sparrow.

  • Behavior

    Dark-eyed Juncos are birds of the ground. They hop around the bases of trees and shrubs in forests or venture out onto lawns looking for fallen seeds. You‚Äôll often hear their high chip notes, given almost absent-mindedly while foraging, or intensifying as they take short, low flights through cover.

  • Habitat

    Dark-eyed Juncos breed in coniferous or mixed-coniferous forests across Canada, the western U.S., and in the Appalachians. During winter you‚Äôll find them in open woodlands, fields, parks, roadsides, and backyards.

Regional Differences

There is a huge range of geographic variation in the Dark-eyed Junco. Among the 15 described races, six forms are easily recognizable in the field and five used to be considered separate species until the 1980s. A field guide is the best place to look for complete illustration of ranges and plumages, but in general there are two widespread forms of the Dark-eyed Junco: ‚Äúslate-colored‚Äù junco of the eastern United States and most of Canada, which is smooth gray above; and ‚ÄúOregon‚Äù junco, found across much of the western U.S., with a dark hood, warm brown back and rufous flanks.
