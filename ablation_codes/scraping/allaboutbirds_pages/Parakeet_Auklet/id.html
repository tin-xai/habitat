Parakeet Auklet Photos and Videos

Breeding

Small auk with round head and very thick, short bill. In breeding plumage, note dark throat. Pale eye, orange bill, and single white streak behind eye visible all year.

© Ken Oeser | Macaulay LibraryAlaska, June 22, 2017

Breeding and nonbreeding

Small auk with very thick, short, orange bill. Nonbreeding birds (right) have white extending up onto the throat; breeding birds have dark throats.

© Mary Trombley | Macaulay LibraryAlaska, June 01, 2017

Breeding adult

Typically selects nesting sites along high cliffs with rocky crevices or within steep grassy slopes where burrowing is possible. Occasionally chooses to use abandoned burrows from previous auklet pairs.

© Benjamin Clock | Macaulay LibraryAlaska, June 12, 2012

Breeding

The Four Keys to ID

  • Size & Shape

    A plump seabird with an unusual rounded bill, thick neck, broad chest, and very short tail.

    Relative Size

    Larger than a Least Auklet, smaller than a Pigeon Guillemot.

    between sparrow and robin
    Measurements
    • Both Sexes
    © Ken Oeser | Macaulay Library
  • Color Pattern

    Breeding adults are blackish above, mostly white below, with orange bill, and wispy white plumes behind the whitish eye. Nonbreeding adults are similar but breast and throat whitish, bill dusky orange, and reduced plumes. Juvenile similar but bill smaller and duskier.

    © Ruben Stoll | Macaulay Library
  • Behavior

    Forages by diving and swimming underwater, using wings for propulsion, to capture fish, jellies, and crustaceans with the bill. Usually solitary at sea. Males “sing” from rocky shores to attract females, which duet with the male of their choice.

    © Nigel Voaden | Macaulay Library
  • Habitat

    Nests in small, loose colonies of a few dozen pairs on rocky shorelines and cliffs of North Pacific islands. Forages in deep, open waters of the North Pacific, often very far from land.

    © Jane Sender | Macaulay Library
