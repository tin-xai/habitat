Horned Grebe

Breeding adult

Horned Grebes in breeding plumage have a chestnut neck and golden head tufts that run in a line along the head, rather than Eared Grebe's black neck and fan-shaped splay of feathers.

© Kyle Blaney | Macaulay LibraryAlberta, June 01, 2015

Horned Grebe

Nonbreeding

Horned Grebes in nonbreeding plumage have a sharp border between the black crown and white cheek. At close range, they have a white tip to the bill whereas Eared Grebe's bill is all dark.

© Alix d'Entremont | Macaulay LibraryNova Scotia, February 21, 2015

Similar Species

Red-necked Grebe

Breeding adult

Red-necked Grebes are larger with longer, stouter bills than Eared Grebes. In breeding plumage they have a white cheek and red neck, unlike Eared's black neck and head.

© Richard Poort | Macaulay LibraryOntario, May 19, 2017
View Full Species Account
Similar Species

Red-necked Grebe

Nonbreeding

Red-necked Grebes are larger and longer necked than Eared Grebes, with a larger bill that is yellowish. In nonbreeding plumage Red-necked has a clean white cheek without the dark ear patch of Eared.

© Samuel Paul Galick | Macaulay LibraryNew Jersey, February 10, 2014

Pied-billed Grebe

Breeding

Pied-billed Grebes are smaller than Eared Grebes with a shorter neck and a thicker, stubbier bill.

© Luke Seitz | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, March 25, 2013

Main Species

Eared Grebe

  • Breeding adult

    Fairly small grebe with peaked head and thin, straight bill. Breeding adult has black head and neck with golden feathers fanning out behind eye. Sides are chestnut.

    © Caroline Lambert | Macaulay LibraryAlberta, May 08, 2019
  • Nonbreeding adult/immature

    Fairly small grebe with small head and thin, straight bill. Black cap extends downward into a smudge on the cheek. Grayish neck accentuates white chin patch.

    © Renato Bagarrão | Macaulay LibraryLisboa, January 22, 2017
  • Breeding adult

    Breeding adults have a fan of golden feathers at the "ear," with a sooty body and cinnamon-red sides. Grebes have lobed rather than fully webbed feet that sit at the rear of their body.

    © Galatee Films | Macaulay LibraryMay 01, 2000
    Not all videos have sound
  • Immature

    Small grebe with slender neck and rounded or peaked head. Immatures have brownish smudged necks.

    © Dorian Anderson | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, February 05, 2020
  • Molting adult

    © Ryan Sanderson | Macaulay LibraryIndiana, April 14, 2018
  • Breeding adults

    Eared Grebes have an elaborate courtship. The dance starts with an advertising call and progresses into a series of dives, feather raising, exaggerated body positions, and "running" across the water with neck extended.

    © Galatee Films | Macaulay LibraryMay 01, 2000
    Not all videos have sound
  • Breeding adult

    Fairly small grebe with slender bill. At distance, breeding adults can appear all dark.

    © Matthew Pendleton | Macaulay LibraryUtah, May 13, 2017
  • Breeding adult

    Often occurs in flocks, sometimes very large during migration (up to hundreds of thousands). Note peaked head and slender bill.

    © Matt Davis | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, April 21, 2017
  • Nonbreeding adult/immature

    In nonbreeding plumage, note peaked or rounded head, gray wash on cheeks, and white chin. Lacks a clear dividing line between hindneck and crown.

    © Peter Hawrylyshyn | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, January 23, 2021
  • Adult and chick

    © Saravana Moorthy | Macaulay LibraryAlberta, June 22, 2014
  • Nonbreeding adult/immature (with Bufflehead)

    Note very different shape compared to ducks (even small ones like the Bufflehead at left): slender neck and small head with short, thin, bill.

    © Paul Fenwick | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, January 13, 2016
  • Immature

    © Jerry McFetridge | Macaulay LibraryBritish Columbia, August 31, 2018
