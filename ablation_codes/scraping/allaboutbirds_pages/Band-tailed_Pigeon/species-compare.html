Rock Pigeon

Adult

Some Rock Pigeons can be all dark, but they lack the Band-tailed's pale band at the tip of the tail, white crescent at the back of the neck, and yellow bill.

© George Chiu | Macaulay LibraryNew York, May 03, 2017

Eurasian Collared-Dove

Adult/immature

Eurasian Collared-Doves are smaller than Band-tailed Pigeons with paler and browner plumage and a long, square-tipped tail. They have a black collar on the back of the neck; Band-tailed has a white crescent.

© Ryan Schain | Macaulay LibraryFlorida, December 12, 2015

Mourning Dove

Adult

Mourning Doves are smaller and browner than Band-tailed Pigeons, with a long, pointed tail.

White-winged Dove

Adult/immature

White-winged Doves are smaller and browner than Band-tailed Pigeons with a white stripe in the wing that is visible both in flight and at rest.

Band-tailed Pigeon

  • Adult (Northern)

    Large pigeon with grayish upperparts and purplish gray underparts. White crescent on back of neck and pale tip to tail are distinctive.

    © Paul Fenwick | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, April 09, 2017
  • Juvenile (Northern)

    Large pigeon with small head and long tail. Young birds lack the white crescent on the neck and have light scalloping on the back.

    © Grace Oliver | Macaulay LibraryWashington, September 24, 2013
  • Adult

    Note the white band and iridescent green scaly-looking feathers on the back of the neck.

    © Brian Sullivan | Macaulay LibraryCalifornia, September 13, 2012
    Not all videos have sound
  • Adult (Northern)

    Large pigeon with thick-based, pointed wings. In flight, look for the light band at the end of the tail.

    © Bridget Spencer | Macaulay LibraryBritish Columbia, June 29, 2017
  • Adult (White-necked)

    © Juan Vargas - Travel By Nature | Macaulay LibraryTolima, November 26, 2020
