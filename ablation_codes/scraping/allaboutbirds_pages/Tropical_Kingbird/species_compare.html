Couch's Kingbird

Couch's Kingbirds are very similar and are best separated by voice. In general Couch's have a shorter and thicker bill than Tropical Kingbirds, but can be difficult to judge in the field.

© Carlos Echeverría | Macaulay LibraryPetén, December 08, 2016

Similar Species

Cassin's Kingbird

Cassin's Kingbirds have a darker gray head, back and breast than Tropical Kingbirds. Cassin's Kingbirds also have a white tip on the tail that Tropical Kingbirds lack.

© Roger Clark | Macaulay LibraryNew Mexico, May 21, 2017
View Full Species Account
Similar Species

Western Kingbird

Western Kingbirds have white outer tail feathers (visible in flight) that Tropical Kingbirds do not have. They also have more gray on the breast than Tropical Kingbirds.

© Gerrit Vyn | Macaulay LibraryAlberta, May 24, 2010

Main Species

Tropical Kingbird

  • Adult/immature

    Large and big-headed flycatcher with a long, heavy bill. Bright yellow below with a whitish throat. The head is gray with a darker gray ear patch.

    © Daniel Irons | Macaulay LibraryAlajuela, March 07, 2019
  • Adult/immature

    Large flycatcher with a long notched tail. The tail is entirely brown unlike Western and Cassin's Kingbird. Bright yellow below with a gray head and darker ear patch.

    © Martin Flack | Macaulay LibraryAlajuela, April 22, 2017
  • Adult/immature

    Like other tyrant flycatchers, Tropical Kingbirds perch on lone branches or wires in open areas where they hunt flying insects.

    © Larry Arbanas | Macaulay LibraryArizona, May 12, 2006
    Not all videos have sound
  • Adult and juvenile

    Large tropical flycatcher that often perches in the open. Bright yellow below with a notched tail.

    © Jorge Eduardo Ruano | Macaulay LibraryOrange Walk, February 12, 2020
  • Adult/immature

    Vocal, big-headed flycatcher with a long notched tail. Yellow below with a whitish throat and a gray head. Note long bill.

    © Larry Manfredi | Macaulay LibraryFlorida, November 19, 2020
  • Adult/immature

    Mainly eats insects, but will forage opportunistically for fruits.

    © Larry Arbanas | Macaulay LibraryArizona, May 12, 2006
    Not all videos have sound
  • Adult/immature

    Found in open country with scattered trees and shrubs.

    © Dodo Colombia | Macaulay LibraryDistrito Capital de Bogotá, June 16, 2016
